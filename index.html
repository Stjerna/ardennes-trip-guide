What's on while we're there — and the one holiday that shuts the shops.

Nightly · 25 minRuns to 22 Aug

Ghost of Berthe · La Roche castle

Every summer evening around 22:00 the castle runs its sound-and-light ghost show: Berthe, daughter of a 10th-century count, walks the ramparts. It runs until 22 August — every single night of our stay. Short, atmospheric, and a magical excuse for a late night if any kids are still standing.

When nightly ±22:00 · chateaudelaroche.be

Sat 15 AugShops closed

Assumption Day — public holiday

15 August is a national holiday in Belgium, and it lands on our first Saturday. Supermarkets run Sunday hours at best; many shops close entirely. Do the big arrival shop on Friday the 14th before 18:30, and expect village fêtes and brocantes around instead.

Plan big shop Fri 14th on arrival · bakeries usually open in the morning

Sat · 20 minEvery other week

Bastogne market

Around a hundred stalls on Rue des Récollets, every other Saturday from 7:00 to 13:00 — produce, cheese, clothes, the works. Our Saturdays are the 15th (a holiday, so likely shifted or skipped) — check bastogne.be for the August dates before planning the grandparents-and-coffee morning.

When alternate Saturdays 7:00–13:00 · Rue des Récollets

The region's must-see: the Battle of the Bulge told through a headphone narration from four personal perspectives, with three cinematic set-pieces. Plan ~2.5 h; ticket includes other Bastogne sites and town parking. The Mardasson Memorial outside is good toddler run-around space.

Free entry, free parking. The change-of-scenery alternative to the villa pool, and a kayak put-in point too.

Kayaking the Ourthe

Classic Ardennes activity from La Roche: rental outfits shuttle you upstream and you paddle calm water back to town. Brandsport (+32 84 41 10 84) and Ardenne Aventures (+32 84 41 19 00) both handle groups. In a dry August the upper Ourthe can run too low — call the day before to confirm they're running, and take a morning slot.

Adventure Valley Durbuy

Belgium's biggest adventure park, next door to Durbuy town: the largest tree-climbing network in Benelux (20 courses), a 566 m zipline, tubing slide, via ferrata and kayaks — plus indoor laser game, bowling and escape rooms for wet days. Most activities open from 110 cm, so the 30-somethings can bring the older kids.

Spa town & Thermes de Spa

The original spa town — the word comes from here. Faded-grandeur arcades, a casino, and hilltop thermal baths reached by funicular (pools, saunas, hammams; day passes with lunch available). Adults-oriented — check the age policy — and your villa already has a pool and jacuzzi, so treat it as an aunts-and-uncles outing.

Vianden Castle (Luxembourg)

The most spectacular castle in the region by a distance: a fully restored fairy-tale hilltop fortress above a lovely river town. Chairlift up for the classic view, €2 audio guide worth every cent, manageable for less mobile relatives. Better than Clervaux if you only do one Luxembourg castle.

Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps

The legendary F1 track, 10 minutes from Spa town. You can often walk the grandstands, see Eau Rouge in person, visit the museum or watch track days. Good news for our week: the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix was 17–19 July, so by mid-August the Stavelot/Coo/Spa area is back to normal — check the track calendar if you want to catch cars running.

Good to know

Cash & fuel

Cash: several favourites are cash-only (the La Roche castle, Baraddur). ATMs are now bank-neutral "CASH points" — find the nearest on cash.be, and top up whenever you're in Bastogne or La Roche. Fuel: noticeably cheaper across the Luxembourg border — fill up on the Clervaux, Vianden or Vennbahn day (Troisvierges is 25 min).

Emergencies & health

The address to give: Randoux 1, Mabompré (Houffalize).

  • 112 — all emergencies, EU-wide
  • 1733 — out-of-hours doctor (evenings/weekends)
  • 070 245 245 — poison control (Centre Antipoisons)
  • 24/7 ER: Hôpital Sainte-Thérèse, Chée d'Houffalize 1, Bastogne — 20 min straight down our road
  • Pharmacy: Gilson, Rue de Liège 3, Houffalize — Mon–Fri 9:00–12:30, 13:30–19:00, Sat morning
  • Night/weekend duty pharmacy: pharmacie.be

Sunscreen, tick tweezers and kids' paracetamol are worth having in the car — pharmacies keep short hours out here.
