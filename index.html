Activities & sights

5 minAges 2–7Rainy-day card

Houtopia

Children's discovery centre in Houffalize: interactive exhibits indoors, a big playground outside, café for the adults. Aimed squarely at your kids' ages — plan 2–3 hours. Free parking 100 m away.

Open daily 10:00–18:00 · Pl. de l'Eglise 17 · +32 61 28 92 05

10 minBook online

Brasserie Chouffe

Home of La Chouffe, the gnome-branded blonde, in the fairy-tale hamlet of Achouffe. Guided tour ends with a tasting; kids get colouring sheets. Tours sell out — book online early and mention your group size. Tavern next door for lunch.

Open daily 10:00–17:00 (18:00 weekends) · chouffe.com

Google ↗
15 minFree

Le Hérou viewpoint

80-metre cliffs above a hairpin bend of the Ourthe near Nadrin — the most dramatic scenery in the area. Short out-and-back viewpoint walks for everyone; longer river loops for the fit. Trails are rooty: baby carrier, not stroller.

Open always · park at the old Belvédère in Nadrin

Google ↗
15 minFree

Barrage de Nisramont

Dam and lake where the two Ourthes meet. Lakeside café by the parking, short shoreline strolls, kayaking on flat water, and a beautiful (steep, 13 km) full-lake loop for serious walkers with good shoes.

Open always · café at the foot of the dam

Google ↗
20 minAll agesRainy-day card

Bastogne War Museum & Mardasson

The region's must-see: the Battle of the Bulge told through a headphone narration from four personal perspectives, with three cinematic set-pieces. Plan ~2.5 h; ticket includes other Bastogne sites and town parking. The Mardasson Memorial outside is good toddler run-around space.

Open daily 9:30–19:00 · Colline du Mardasson 5 · +32 61 21 02 20

Google ↗
25 minToddler gold

Le Parc à Gibier, La Roche

Forest wildlife park with deer, boar, wolves and lynx, plus a playground and café. Healthy, well-kept animals and generous enclosures. It's on a hillside — steep in places, so carrier over stroller.

Open daily 10:00–19:00 · +32 470 10 80 78

Google ↗
25 min

Château Féodal, La Roche

Ruined 11th-century castle crowning the prettiest river town in the Ardennes. Kids clamber the ramparts, free archery activity, great views over town. Cash for entry and the castle bar. Combine with wildlife park + ice cream for a full La Roche day.

Open daily 10:00–18:00 · +32 84 41 13 42

Google ↗
25 minAdults + teens

Kayaking the Ourthe

Classic Ardennes activity from La Roche: rental outfits shuttle you upstream and you paddle calm water back to town. Brandsport (+32 84 41 10 84) and Ardenne Aventures (+32 84 41 19 00) both handle groups; book a morning slot in summer for water levels and parking.

Season daily ~10:00–15:00 starts · not for the under-4s

Google ↗
30 minAges 3–10

Parc Chlorophylle

Forest discovery-and-play park at Dochamps: treetop walkway, themed play stations and art installations along an easy trail, restaurant on site. Families with kids exactly your ages rate it the best value day in the region. Book online for a small discount.

Open daily 9:30–18:30 · +32 84 37 87 74

Google ↗
30 minClosed Wed

Clervaux Castle (Luxembourg)

White hilltop castle above a pretty valley town: Edward Steichen's UNESCO-listed "Family of Man" photo exhibition, a Battle of the Bulge museum, and a castle-model collection. Easy strolling and good cafés — ideal pace for the older generation.

Open Thu–Tue 10:00–17:00 · +352 28 41 98

Google ↗
50 minRides from 85 cm

Plopsaland Ardennes, Coo

Small, gentle theme park genuinely built for pre-schoolers — many rides open from 85 cm, whole park doable in a day, next to the Coo waterfalls and a cable car viewpoint. Closes 18:00, so arrive at opening. Park just outside the entrance to save a few euros.

Open daily 10:00–18:00 in season · plopsacoo.be

Google ↗
1 h 10Big day tripRainy-day card

Domain of the Caves of Han

Belgium's most spectacular caves — vast chambers reached by a charming old tram — plus a safari park with bears, wolves and bison. Cave tour is walkable with a 4-year-old; bring the carrier for the little one and warm layers (it's cold underground).

Open daily from 10:00 · grotte-de-han.be · +32 84 37 72 13

Further afield

45 minBest all-ages add-on

Durbuy + Topiary Park

"The smallest town in the world" — cobbled lanes, stone houses, riverside castle, and the prettiest streets in the Ardennes. Next door, the Topiary Park (€5, flat and easy) is full of animal-shaped hedges toddlers love spotting. Slots naturally onto a Chlorophylle day.

Topiary Park daily 10:00–18:00 · paid parking out front

Google ↗
50 minAdults' half day

Spa town & Thermes de Spa

The original spa town — the word comes from here. Faded-grandeur arcades, a casino, and hilltop thermal baths reached by funicular (pools, saunas, hammams; day passes with lunch available). Adults-oriented — check the age policy — and your villa already has a pool and jacuzzi, so treat it as an aunts-and-uncles outing.

Thermes daily from 9:00 · +32 87 77 25 60

Google ↗
50 minWow factor

Vianden Castle (Luxembourg)

The most spectacular castle in the region by a distance: a fully restored fairy-tale hilltop fortress above a lovely river town. Chairlift up for the classic view, €2 audio guide worth every cent, manageable for less mobile relatives. Better than Clervaux if you only do one Luxembourg castle.

Open daily 10:00–18:00 · +352 83 41 08 1

Google ↗
55 minCheck GP dates

Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps

The legendary F1 track, 10 minutes from Spa town. Outside race weekends you can often walk the grandstands, see Eau Rouge in person, visit the museum or watch track days. If the Belgian Grand Prix (late July) overlaps your week, the whole Stavelot/Coo area will be jammed — check before driving that way.

Open daily 9:00–18:00 · +32 87 29 37 00

Google ↗
1 h 10Optional city day

Luxembourg City

UNESCO old town perched on cliffs above the fairy-tale Grund quarter — free elevator between the levels, café terraces everywhere, and public transport is free nationwide. Only worth the drive if the group wants one proper city fix; the Ardennes is the star of this trip.

Tip park at a P+R and ride in free

Golden rule for a group this size: always call ahead, even for casual places. Ardennes kitchens are small, many close Monday–Wednesday, and most stop serving by 20:30–21:00.

Morning runs

Bakeries

Very Belgian tip: phone a day ahead and pre-order — 15 croissants at 8 am is a normal request, and it beats the morning scramble. Staff answer in French but manage fine in English.

5 minDaily from 6:30

Valentine · Houffalize

The easiest daily run: pâtisserie with seating, coffee, and legendary filled pains au chocolat (vanilla cream + banana gets rave reviews). Also scoops ice cream, so the croissant run can escalate. Try the citron meringue.

Hours daily 6:30–18:30 · Pl. de l'Eglise 15 · +32 61 32 11 09

Supermarket freezer

For the villa freezer

IJsboerke is the classic Belgian brand in every freezer aisle — vanilla and mokka are national nostalgia. Also grab anything labelled glace fermière / artisanale (local farm tubs usually beat the big brands) and a bag of Callebaut chocolate callets to melt over everything.

Where Spar/AD Delhaize in Houffalize, 5 min

For the 30-somethings

Gyms & biking

Houffalize is Belgium's mountain-biking capital — it hosts the UCI MTB World Cup, and marked trails start in town, with a mountain-bike route passing 100 m from the villa. The hills are real: e-MTBs earn their rental fee here.

20 min24/7 · day pass ~€9

Basic-Fit · Bastogne

The reliable option: open around the clock, full equipment, day pass from the self-service kiosk at the entrance — no membership needed.

Where Rue de Marche 167

5 minRoad cycling

Côte de Saint-Roch

The famous Liège–Bastogne–Liège wall climbs straight out of Houffalize town centre — short, brutally steep, lined with painted names on race day. Riding it is a rite of passage in this valley.

Bonus coffee at Rouleur, the cyclists' café in town

Vennbahn rail-trail

One of Europe's longest railway cycle paths starts at Troisvierges — gentle rail-grade gradients through lovely country, ideal for a mixed-ability or e-bike day when the MTB crowd needs recovery legs.

Start Troisvierges (LU), ~25 min drive
